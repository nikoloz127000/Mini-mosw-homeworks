Golden State Warriors superstar Stephen Curry achieved another significant career milestone when he surpassed 25,000 career points on March 9 in the Warriors’ 115-110 victory over the Detroit Pistons at Chase Center. Curry reached this landmark with a three-pointer in the third quarter, and by doing so became the 26th player in NBA history to reach or surpass the mark. This is another important achievement for the newly-minted Olympic champion, with fellow OG’s LeBron James and Kevin Durant reaching 50,000 points and 30,000 points, respectively. The game continues to highlight these three global superstars as they continue to dominate the game even with their advanced age. The 36-year-old sharpshooter finished the game with 32 points, leading the Warriors to their 11th win in 13 games and 11-1 since acquiring Jimmy Butler. Teammate Draymond Green ensured the game ball was preserved for Curry, highlighting the significance of the achievement. Curry's milestone underscores his enduring excellence and pivotal role in the Warriors' success over the years. As he continues to ascend the all-time scoring list, his legacy as one of the game's greatest shooters remains firmly intact.
